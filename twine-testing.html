Twine Happy-Path Testing for Accessibility

Twine is a hypertext programming language/editor. The most basic form of a Twine story involves no logic at all, and is simply constructed from text passages, each of which typically contain links (usually highlighted words or phrases) to other passages. Logic can be added with macros (essentially simple functions), such as to allow the author to change text on-screen based on a timer or user interaction.

Links can be clicked to move one around the story. When the link is clicked, a new passage is visited. More complicated usages involve injecting the contents of a passage into the current passage with the display macro.

You also can, in most Twine versions, use the on-page back and forward arrows to rewind and fast-forward the state of the story. Doing so does not consume time or register as an in-world action in any way. In some, but not all formats, you can also use the browser back and forward buttons to rewind and fast-forward.
